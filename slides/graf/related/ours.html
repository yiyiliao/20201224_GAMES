## 3D Representations #### Generative Radiance Fields
 
  • Continuous representation, multi-view consistent
  • High image fidelity, low memory consumption
  • Our approach is inspired by representation recently introduced for 3D reconstruction tasks, namely neural radiance fields
  • In contrast to voxel-based representations, we generate the radiance fields of scenes.
  • But actually, what !is! a "radiance field"? A radiance field is a continuous function, that maps a 3D location and a viewing direction to a color value
  • More specifically, we map a 3D location and a viewing direction to a color value and a volume density
  • Then, similar to PlatonicGAN, we can use volumetric rendering techniques to project the 3D representation to an image
  • This allows us to directly generate the 3D object, as opposed to the abstract 3D feature from HoloGAN. So in contrast to HoloGAN we can omit a learnable projection.
  • The generated images of our approach are multi-view consistent as indicated by the good reconstructions from COLMAP. And, as our representation is a continuous function, we obtain high image fidelity and good scalability to higher resolutions.
